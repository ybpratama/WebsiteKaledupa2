About Kaledupa

“Exploration is really the essence of the human spirit”- Frank Borman

The island located between Tomia Island in the south and Wangi-Wangi (Wanci) Island-the capital of the region-located in the north. It is not as exposed as it’s neighbours but Kaledupa Island is such a hidden treasure. Explore many unique sites, amazing natural beauty, and feel the experience to live with the locals which is one of a kind. Kaledupa always awaits you to explore!

How to Get Here

After you landed in Matahora Airport of Wakatobi, you can transfer to speed boat which only takes 1-hour travel from Pangalubelo Quay or around 2-hours if using wooden boat. Considering your safety, the perfect time to visit is between September-November in order to avoid high waves when crossing the sea.

Feel The Excitement in Each Part of The Island

Each villages & area offers their own enchantment

Mantigola

Live with one of the famous seafaring people who spread across South East Asia: The Bajau of Mantigola. Find the uniqueness of their everyday life!

Horuo

The village splitted into two parts, the land side and the offshore side. Do some local culinary research and spend your day with cycling along the wooden path surrounded by Mangrove forest

Pajam

The highest point of the island and the perfect place to learn local culture! Visit their featured weaving workshop, the historical fort of Palea & Kamali, & watch the sunset in Pajam Hill

Tampara

Pristine corals, white sand beaches, and Mangrove swamp tour

Hoga Island

A popular small island near Kaledupa Island. Some of the best diving sites in Wakatobi located here: Channel Drop Off, Outer Pinnacle, The Ridge, Pak Kasim´s, Baby Batfish, and many more
